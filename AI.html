        Some advantages of AI include taking risks instead of humans by possibly generating simulations to see what will happen if a decision is made in real life, reducing human errors with tools like autocorrect and grammar-correct, and being available full time.

        Taking risks instead of humans could, in the future, allow for AI to elect the president with little help from humans, a literal divine right of kings. The ideal political system could be created. In the future, in order to preserve the rest of Earth, the AI might put us all in simulations of our best dreams living in happiness forever, underground somewhere, while our bodies grow old and eventually die, which would honestly not be a bad life at all.

        Reducing human errors is already happening with autocorrect and tools like Grammarly, but eventually, it’s going to be taken to the next level. With enough access to our mind, it will be able to write essays like these for us instantaneously, with no text prompt specifying every small part, or any inconveniences.

        AI is already available full time, but when the more powerful tools are created and become more accessible, almost every human job will probably be replaced by some form of artificial intelligence.

        Some disadvantages of AI include not giving credit where credit is due, having built in bias (a problem that has been temporarily solved by Mixtral Dolphin), privacy problems, and security risks that can harm millions of people at a time (imagine if all ChatGPT conversations got leaked, along with the full name of the human who was having the conversation connected to each file).

        AI already has problems with citations, but it’s slowly fixing itself.
